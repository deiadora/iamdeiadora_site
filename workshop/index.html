Quantum currency workshop · step 4 · wave · first cube

The canvas before the choice.
All faces present. No direction yet committed.

In quantum mechanics, a wave function holds all possible states simultaneously until the moment of observation collapses it into one. The first cube is the geometry of that superposition — six faces, each a different surface of the same system, none yet chosen as primary. The financial model canvas is how you see all six faces at once, before committing to any single future.

"The path never lets the wave collapse into form. What revenue streams, models, or structures has this system been circling without committing?"

This is not a business plan. A business plan is a commitment. The canvas is an observation — the full architecture of what this system is and could be, mapped without judgment, before Step 5 chooses a direction. The irreducible problem from Step 3 sits at the centre. Everything else is organised around it.

The canvas — six faces of the financial system

Revenue streams

What this system earns and how — current, potential, and dormant.

Cost structure

What it costs to operate — fixed, variable, hidden, and personal.

Personal layer

The founder's own financial needs and the degree to which they're being met.

Unchosen potential

Revenue streams circled but not committed to. Models considered but not tested. Superpositions waiting to collapse.

The irreducible problem

From Step 3 — the real constraint at the centre of the economic system.

The economic engine

The value created, for whom, and why they pay for it. The core of the torus.

What is the full shape of this system — including the parts that haven't been looked at directly yet?

The canvas is not for polishing. It is for seeing.

Why the canvas comes before the scenarios

Step 5 will model three futures simultaneously — contraction, steady-state, expansion. But you cannot model futures from an incomplete map of the present. The canvas is the prerequisite for the scenarios. A team that skips the canvas and goes straight to futures is optimising without knowing what they're optimising from. The canvas forces honesty about the full architecture — including the unchosen potential and the personal layer that most financial models exclude.

The unchosen potential face

This is the face of the canvas that most teams find most uncomfortable — and most generative. Unchosen potential is the wave function that has never been observed. The revenue stream that keeps being mentioned in meetings but never built. The service that clients have asked for repeatedly but the team has been "not ready" to offer. The partnership that would unlock a new model but requires a conversation that keeps being deferred. Mapping the unchosen is not a commitment to pursue it. It is an acknowledgment that the system already knows it is there.

Canvas signals — what to listen for

The wave is present

The team can name unchosen potential immediately and specifically. There's energy in the room when it's named — not anxiety, but recognition. The canvas feels like seeing the system whole for the first time.

The canvas is frozen

The team can only see current reality — no unchosen potential surfaces. The personal layer is actively resisted or minimised. This is often the signal that the Step 3 irreducible hasn't fully landed yet.

The canvas is leaking

Many revenue streams identified, none fully resourced. Unchosen potential proliferating without constraint. The system is generating ideas faster than it can commit to them. Step 5 will need to apply discernment to the wave before it becomes noise.

The personal layer — why it belongs on the canvas

Most business model canvases have no personal layer. This one does — because the torus from Step 1 established that personal and business finances are one system. A founder who maps a business model without mapping their own financial needs is building a model that may be economically coherent on paper and personally unsustainable in practice. The personal layer asks: what does the person running this system need to earn to be sustainable — and is the current model capable of generating that, now or at scale?

What this step tends to produce

The most common revelation in Step 4 is that the unchosen potential is already the answer to the irreducible problem from Step 3. The revenue stream that hasn't been built is the one that would close the torus. The model that hasn't been committed to is the one that addresses the root. The canvas makes this visible — not by analysis, but by observation. The system already knew. The canvas just made it legible.

Map the full architecture of this system — personal and business together, current and potential, known and unchosen. Nothing here is a commitment. The canvas is an act of observation, not planning. Write quickly. The first things that surface are usually the most honest ones.

The financial model canvas

Map all six faces. Include the personal layer. Include the unchosen. Write from observation — what is actually here, not what should be here.

Revenue streams

Current, recurring, one-off, potential. What does and could this system earn?

Cost structure

Fixed, variable, hidden. What leaves the system — including costs paid personally for the business.

Personal layer

What does the person or people running this system need to earn to be genuinely sustainable?

Unchosen potential

Revenue streams circled but not built. Models considered but not committed to. What the system keeps returning to without acting on.

The irreducible problem

From Step 3 — write it here exactly as named. This is the centre of the canvas.

The economic engine

The value created, for whom, and why they pay for it. The core of the torus in one or two sentences.

What is the system ready to choose?

The Sower question at step 4

Return to the lens: what kind of ground is this canvas growing in?

A canvas mapped in good soil tends to surface the unchosen potential readily — the system is coherent enough to see its own possibilities without defensiveness. A canvas mapped in thorny ground tends to proliferate — every face generates three more, and the unchosen list never ends. If the canvas feels overwhelming rather than clarifying, the discernment step (3) may need to be revisited before Step 5.

The wave is present. The cube holds all faces. Step 5 is where one direction gets chosen — and the others don't disappear. They wait, as superpositions, for the next cycle. The Wonka elevator can always return.
